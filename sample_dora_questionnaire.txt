--- SECTION 1: ICT RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK ---

Q1. Do you maintain a documented ICT risk management framework?
A: Yes. Our ICT risk management framework is documented and board-approved (last reviewed
   January 2026). It covers risk identification, assessment, treatment, monitoring and review.
   The framework is aligned with ISO 27001 and references DORA requirements.

Q2. Is the ICT risk management framework reviewed at least annually?
A: Yes. Annual review conducted by CISO and approved by the Board of Directors.
   Last review: January 2026. Next review: January 2027.

Q3. Do you maintain an ICT asset inventory?
A: Yes. Full ICT asset inventory maintained in ServiceNow CMDB, covering hardware,
   software, data assets and third-party ICT dependencies. Updated in real time
   via automated discovery agents. Last manual reconciliation: February 2026.

--- SECTION 2: ICT INCIDENT MANAGEMENT ---

Q4. Do you have a documented ICT incident management policy?
A: Yes. Incident management policy covers detection, classification, escalation,
   response, reporting and post-incident review.

Q5. How do you classify ICT incidents?
A: Three-tier classification:
   - P1 (Major): Full service outage or confirmed data breach affecting multiple clients
   - P2 (Significant): Degraded service or potential security incident
   - P3 (Minor): Limited impact, no client effect

Q6. Do you have reporting procedures aligned with DORA timelines?
A: Partially. We have client notification SLAs (P1: 2 hours). We are currently
   implementing formal regulatory reporting procedures aligned with DORA Article 19
   (initial notification within 4 hours, intermediate within 72 hours, final within 1 month).
   Expected completion: Q2 2026.
   NOTE: This is an acknowledged gap being remediated.

Q7. Do you maintain an ICT incident log?
A: Yes. All incidents logged in ServiceNow with full timeline, impact assessment,
   root cause analysis and remediation actions. Log retained for minimum 5 years.

--- SECTION 3: DIGITAL OPERATIONAL RESILIENCE TESTING ---

Q8. Do you conduct regular operational resilience testing?
A: Yes. Annual penetration testing (Fox-IT, external). Last conducted September 2025.
   Additionally: bi-annual vulnerability assessments, quarterly DR failover tests.

Q9. Have you conducted Threat-Led Penetration Testing (TLPT)?
A: No. TLPT has not been conducted. We are assessing whether TLPT is required
   based on the size and systemic importance criteria. This is under review with
   our legal advisors. Expected decision: Q3 2026.

Q10. What is the scope of your resilience testing?
A: External pentest covers all external-facing services and APIs.
   Internal assessment covers internal network segmentation and lateral movement paths.
   DR testing covers full failover to secondary AWS region (EU-West-2).

--- SECTION 4: ICT THIRD-PARTY RISK MANAGEMENT ---

Q11. Do you maintain a register of all ICT third-party service arrangements?
A: Yes. Third-party ICT register maintained in GRC tool (ServiceNow GRC).
   Currently 12 material ICT third-party arrangements documented.
   Register reviewed quarterly.

Q12. What contractual provisions do you offer for DORA compliance?
A: Standard contract includes:
   - Audit rights: Client may audit or commission audit annually with 30 days notice
   - SLA: 99.9% availability guaranteed; financial penalties for breach
   - Data portability: Full data export in standard formats within 30 days of termination
   - Business continuity: Minimum 12-month notice for service discontinuation
   - Sub-processor changes: Notification to clients 60 days in advance

Q13. Do you have documented exit strategies for critical ICT services?
A: Yes. Exit strategy documented for all critical ICT dependencies (AWS, Cloudflare).
   Exit strategy for client offboarding: documented data migration procedure,
   30-day parallel running option, full data export and deletion certificate.

Q14. Are you aware of whether you may qualify as a Critical ICT Third-Party Provider (CTPP)?
A: We have reviewed the CTPP designation criteria (Article 31 DORA / ESA assessment).
   Based on our current client base and market share in the Netherlands, we do not
   believe we meet the systemic importance threshold for CTPP designation.
   We will monitor our position as our client base grows.

--- SECTION 5: INFORMATION SHARING ---

Q15. Do you participate in any information sharing arrangements regarding cyber threats?
A: Yes. Member of ISAC FS-ISAC Netherlands and share threat intelligence via the
   Dutch Financial Sector CERT (FSC). Threat intelligence integrated into our
   SIEM (Splunk) via automated feeds.
