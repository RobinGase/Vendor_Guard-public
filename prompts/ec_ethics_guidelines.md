# Ethical Guidelines for Trustworthy AI — European Commission (2019)

## Context
Published by the High-Level Expert Group on AI. Predecessor to ALTAI; foundational principles still referenced in EU AI policy.

## Four ethical principles (grounded in fundamental rights)

### 1. Respect for human autonomy
- AI augments human capabilities, does not replace human agency
- Users retain control over their decisions
- No manipulative design patterns

### 2. Prevention of harm
- AI systems avoid causing harm to individuals or society
- Vulnerability of affected users considered
- Trade-offs between benefits and risks explicitly addressed

### 3. Fairness
- Absence of unfair bias in data, algorithms, and outcomes
- Equal and just treatment of all individuals
- Affected parties have access to redress

### 4. Explicability
- AI processes and decisions are transparent and understandable
- If full explainability is not achievable, mitigating measures are in place
- Documentation of known limitations

## Assessment focus for vendor evaluation
1. Does the vendor's AI documentation reference the EC Ethics Guidelines?
2. Are there design choices that demonstrably respect user autonomy?
3. Is there a harm assessment process?
4. Are fairness metrics defined and monitored?
5. Is there documentation on explainability limitations and mitigations?
