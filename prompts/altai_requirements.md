# ALTAI — Assessment List for Trustworthy AI (EU HLEG, 2020)

## The 7 Key Requirements for Trustworthy AI

### 1. Human Agency and Oversight
- Fundamental rights impact assessment conducted
- Users are informed and can contest AI decisions
- Human oversight mechanisms in place
- AI does not undermine user autonomy

### 2. Technical Robustness and Safety
- System is accurate under normal and adversarial conditions
- Fallback plans exist for AI failures
- Security against attacks (adversarial examples, data poisoning)
- System is reproducible and reliable

### 3. Privacy and Data Governance
- Privacy by design implemented
- Data minimization and purpose limitation
- Data subjects can exercise their rights
- Data quality assurance processes

### 4. Transparency
- AI system decisions are explainable to affected parties
- Appropriate level of explainability for the use case
- Documentation of system capabilities and limitations
- Users know when interacting with an AI

### 5. Diversity, Non-discrimination and Fairness
- Bias testing conducted across protected characteristics
- Accessibility for users with disabilities considered
- Stakeholder participation in design

### 6. Societal and Environmental Wellbeing
- Environmental impact of AI system assessed
- Societal impact considered (labor, social cohesion)
- Democratic values and rule of law respected

### 7. Accountability
- Auditability mechanisms in place
- Clear responsibility allocation (who is accountable for AI decisions)
- Redress mechanisms for those affected by AI decisions
- Regulatory compliance verified

## Key questions to assess
1. Is there a fundamental rights impact assessment?
2. Are AI decisions explainable to end users?
3. Has bias been tested across protected characteristics?
4. Who is accountable for AI decisions and errors?
5. Is there a redress mechanism for affected individuals?
6. Has the environmental impact been assessed?
