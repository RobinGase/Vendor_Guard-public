# CBW — Cyberbeveiligingswet (Dutch NIS2 Implementation)

## Context
The CBW is the Dutch national implementation of the NIS2 Directive, applicable to entities operating in the Netherlands. It adds Dutch-specific enforcement and sector designations.

## Key additions over NIS2
- Dutch sectors designated as "essential": energy, transport, banking, financial market infrastructure, health, drinking water, wastewater, digital infrastructure, ICT service management, public administration, space
- Enforcement by sector-specific authorities (e.g., DNB for finance, RDI for digital)
- Mandatory registration with NCSC (Nationaal Cyber Security Centrum)
- Dutch incident reporting goes to NCSC in addition to EU requirements

## Assessment focus
1. Is the vendor registered or aware of CBW applicability to their services?
2. Are they compliant with the designated sector authority requirements?
3. Do they have a Dutch-language incident reporting procedure?
4. Is there a contact point for NCSC reporting?

## Relationship to NIS2
All NIS2 Article 21 measures apply. CBW adds the Dutch enforcement layer. Assess NIS2 compliance first; then flag any CBW-specific gaps.
