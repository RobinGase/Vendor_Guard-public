# NIS2 Directive — Article 21 Security Measures

## Required measures for essential/important entities and their suppliers

### Risk management
- Documented cybersecurity risk management policy
- Regular risk assessments

### Incident handling
- Incident detection and response capabilities
- Reporting obligations: significant incidents within 24h (early warning), 72h (notification), 1 month (final report)

### Business continuity
- Backup and recovery procedures
- Crisis management plans
- Disaster recovery tested regularly

### Supply chain security
- Security measures addressing risks from third-party relationships
- Contractual security requirements for suppliers

### Network and information systems security
- Network segmentation and access controls
- Vulnerability management and patching

### Access control and authentication
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) mandatory
- Privileged access management

### Cryptography and encryption
- Encryption policies for data in transit and at rest

### Human resources security
- Security awareness training for all staff
- Background checks for critical roles

## Key questions to assess
1. Is there an incident response plan with NIS2-compliant reporting timelines?
2. Is MFA enforced across all systems?
3. Are there documented backup and recovery procedures?
4. Has the vendor conducted a supply chain risk assessment?
5. What security training is provided to staff?
