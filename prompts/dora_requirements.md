# DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU 2022/2554)

## Scope
Applies to financial entities and their critical ICT third-party providers (CTPPs).

## Key pillars

### ICT risk management (Articles 5-16)
- Documented ICT risk management framework
- ICT asset inventory (hardware, software, data)
- ICT-related incident management policy
- Business continuity policy for ICT services

### ICT-related incident reporting (Articles 17-23)
- Classification of incidents by severity
- Major incident reporting to competent authority within 4h (initial), 72h (intermediate), 1 month (final)
- Incident log maintained

### Digital operational resilience testing (Articles 24-27)
- Basic testing: vulnerability assessments, network assessments, gap analyses annually
- Advanced testing (TLPT): Threat-Led Penetration Testing for significant entities every 3 years

### ICT third-party risk management (Articles 28-44)
- Register of all ICT third-party service arrangements
- Pre-contract due diligence
- Contractual provisions: SLAs, audit rights, data portability, termination, business continuity
- Exit strategies documented

### Critical ICT third-party provider (CTPP) status
- Designated by ESAs (EBA, EIOPA, ESMA)
- Subject to direct oversight framework

## Key questions to assess
1. Does the vendor have a documented ICT risk management framework?
2. Is there an incident classification and reporting procedure aligned with DORA timelines?
3. Are penetration tests / TLPT conducted and documented?
4. Does the vendor maintain an ICT asset inventory?
5. Are contractual provisions DORA-compliant (audit rights, exit strategy, SLA)?
6. Is the vendor designated or potentially designatable as a CTPP?
