# EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)

## Risk classification
- **Prohibited AI**: social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance in public spaces, subliminal manipulation
- **High-risk AI**: AI in critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice
- **Limited risk**: chatbots, deepfake generators (transparency obligations)
- **Minimal risk**: spam filters, AI in video games (no specific obligations)

## High-risk AI system obligations (Article 9-17)
1. **Risk management system**: continuous process throughout lifecycle (Art. 9)
2. **Data governance**: training, validation, testing data requirements; bias mitigation (Art. 10)
3. **Technical documentation**: detailed documentation before market placement (Art. 11)
4. **Record-keeping**: automatic logging of events for traceability (Art. 12)
5. **Transparency**: users informed they are interacting with AI (Art. 13)
6. **Human oversight**: design enabling effective human oversight; override capability (Art. 14)
7. **Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity**: performance metrics documented; adversarial robustness (Art. 15)

## General-purpose AI (GPAI) obligations (Articles 51-56)
- Technical documentation for GPAI models
- Copyright compliance policy
- Systemic risk assessment for very capable models (>10^25 FLOPs)

## Conformity assessment
- Self-assessment or third-party audit depending on risk level
- CE marking required for high-risk AI placed on EU market
- Registration in EU database for high-risk AI

## Key questions to assess
1. What is the risk classification of this AI system?
2. Is there a documented risk management system?
3. Is training data documented and bias mitigation described?
4. Is there an automatic logging / audit trail mechanism?
5. Can users identify they are interacting with AI?
6. Is there a human override / intervention mechanism?
7. Has a conformity assessment been conducted?
8. Is the system registered in the EU AI database (if high-risk)?
